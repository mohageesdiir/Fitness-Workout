Barbell Curl

How to do it

Stand tall with feet hip-width apart and grip the barbell shoulder-width with palms up...

Muscles worked

Primary: Biceps brachii. Secondary: Brachialis, brachioradialis...

Coaching tips

Keep shoulders down and back, don’t let elbows drift forward. Use a 2–3 second lower.

Incline Bench Press

How to do it

Set bench to 30–45°. Unrack with wrists stacked over elbows. Lower the bar to the upper chest with elbows ~45–60° from the body. Press up, driving feet into the floor and keeping shoulder blades retracted.

Muscles worked

Primary: Upper pectorals (clavicular head). Secondary: Anterior deltoids, triceps. Stabilizers: Scapular retractors and core.

Tricep Pushdown

How to do it

Stand close to the cable, elbows pinned to your sides. Start with forearms up, then extend the elbows to push the bar down until arms are straight. Control the return without letting elbows drift forward.

Muscles worked

Primary: Triceps (all heads). Secondary: Forearm extensors for grip. Core stabilizes posture through the movement.

Coaching tips

Use a full lockout without hyperextending. Keep wrists neutral. For long head bias, use a rope and finish with a slight external rotation.

Shoulder Press

How to do it

Start with dumbbells at shoulder height, palms forward. Brace your core and press overhead, finishing with biceps near ears. Lower under control to the start without letting elbows collapse inward.

Muscles worked

Primary: Anterior and medial deltoids. Secondary: Triceps, upper traps, serratus. Core stabilizes especially when standing.

Coaching tips

Keep ribs down to avoid over-arching. Press slightly forward then back to finish “head through.” Choose a range that keeps shoulders pain-free.

Squats

How to do it

Set feet shoulder-width and brace. Sit hips down and back while keeping knees tracking over toes. Descend to your available depth with control, then drive through midfoot to stand tall.

Muscles worked

Primary: Quadriceps and glutes. Secondary: Hamstrings, calves, spinal erectors. Core and upper back stabilize the load.

Coaching tips

Brace before each rep, keep chest up without overextending. Drive knees out to match toe angle. Use shoes with stable soles; progress range before load.
